General Duty of Care

All painters and clients must comply with the Health and Safety at Work Act 2015. Painters are responsible for working safely and identifying hazards.

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

Appropriate PPE must be used where required, including masks, gloves, eye protection, and fall protection where applicable.

Hazard Identification

Hazards such as working at height, fumes, confined spaces, and electrical risks must be identified and managed before work begins.

Site Safety

Clients must ensure reasonable site safety, including access, ventilation, and protection of occupants where painting work is undertaken.

Important: Immediate Painters provides guidance only. Responsibility for compliance remains with the painter and the client.

Health & Safety Requirements

All painters working through Immediate Painters must meet New Zealand Health & Safety expectations. This includes safe work practices, appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE), and compliance with site-specific safety requirements.

Immediate Painters promotes a culture of safety, accountability, and professionalism across all painting projects.
